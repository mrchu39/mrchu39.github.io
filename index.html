Research Experience

 

Postbac Researcher for the Zwicky Transient Facility

July 2020 – Present

California Institute of Technology

Research with ZTF includes automating classifications from transients based on spectroscopic and photometric data from several sources (including the P200 telescope on Palomar) and submitting classifications to the Transient Name Server with a 24 hour window. Individual work includes analyzing the geographic concentration of galaxies with known redshifts, providing valuable insight for LIGO in locating sources of gravitational wave events.

 

Research Assistant for Prof. Alexei Filippenko Group

March 2020 – Present

University of California, Berkeley

Regular data collection, primarily photometric and spectropolarimetric images of Type Ia supernovae taken with the Lick and Keck Observatories. Applied image analysis to extract spectropolarimetric data on archival and current datasets to expand understanding of the properties of Type Ia supernovae. Understanding these properties corroborates their utility as metrics to measure dark energy and the expansion of the universe.

 

Research Assistant for Prof. Saul Perlmutter Group (with Dr. Cikota)

September 2018 – September 2021

Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory

Research demonstrating that certain peculiar polarimetric properties observed of Type Ia supernovae are implicit in galactic dust rather than circumstellar dust, thereby narrowing the mystery of the mechanism that triggers the explosions. Work involved identifying potential targets, creating finding charts and submitting observation requests, and compiling polarimetry data from the observations using Python scripts I helped develop. The outcome of this work was submitted as a publication to MNRAS.

Internships/Student Work

 

Electro-Optical Intern

May 2020 – August 2020

Space Dynamics Laboratory – Civil and Commercial Space

Research, testing, and calibration of equipment used on the small satellites engineered and manufactured by SDL. Worked specifically within the Civil and Commercial Space on calibration of the Roman (previously WFIRST) telescope. The work involved designing and engineering test equipment, as well as using said equipment to develop calibration data that allows SmallSats to operate more accurately. We determined the root cause of calibration error using scanning electron metrology and modified testing setup to increase level of precision. Calibration data was compiled and reports were circulated around other organizations that operate the Roman mission, including NASA and JPL.
